# Project Nexus: AI-Enhanced NDIS Support Platform

This repository contains planning materials for **Project Nexus**, an extension of the Nexis365 platform tailored for the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS). The initiative aims to streamline service delivery, empower participants and providers, and support a sustainable NDIS through responsible use of data and AI.

## Problem & Opportunity
- The NDIS supports more than 650,000 participants, yet providers and participants still face complex processes, administrative overheads, and coordination gaps.
- These challenges slow service delivery and limit participant choice and control, highlighting the need for innovation that aligns with the Scheme's mission of enabling people with disability to pursue their goals.

## Solution Overview
- Cloud-based platform that extends Nexis365 with NDIS‑specific case management, rostering, invoicing, and compliance features.
- AI-driven insights automate routine tasks, forecast participant needs, and match supports, freeing staff time for quality care.

## NDIS Alignment & Value Proposition
- **Participants:** increased choice, control, and goal tracking through easier communication and support coordination.
- **Providers:** reduced paperwork, improved compliance management, and more time for client-facing services.
- **NDIA/Government:** fosters a diverse, innovative market and potential system-wide efficiency gains.

## Innovation and Competitive Advantage
- Leverages NDIA Digital Partnership Program APIs for secure integration with NDIA systems.
- AI features proactively flag risks, predict service gaps, and personalise recommendations.
- Designed specifically for NDIS workflows, differentiating it from generic management software.

## Co-Design with Participants
- Commitment to co-design workshops and continuous feedback with people with disability, their families, and providers.
- Pilot treated as a co-design exercise, with diverse participants shaping features, accessibility, and training materials.

## Pilot Plan (20–50 Participants)
- Partner with a reputable NDIS provider to run a 6‑month pilot following an 8‑month development phase.
- Objectives include validating functionality, measuring outcomes (e.g. admin time reduction, satisfaction), and refining the product before scaling.
- Support and training provided for participants and staff, with a helpdesk for rapid issue resolution.

## Legal & Compliance Strategy
- Adheres to the NDIS Code of Conduct, the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), and NDIS Commission requirements.
- Data stored securely in Australia with encryption and role‑based access control; no disclosure without informed consent.
- AI governance follows Australia’s AI Ethics Principles—human‑centred values, fairness, transparency, accountability, and contestability.

## Implementation Roadmap
1. **Months 1–2:** Requirements gathering and architecture design with co-design input.
2. **Months 3–5:** Build core features, AI module, and NDIA API integrations.
3. **Month 6:** Internal alpha testing, security audit, and compliance check.
4. **Month 7:** Beta release to a small group for feedback.
5. **Month 8:** Finalise MVP for pilot; begin pilot preparations.
6. **Months 9–12:** Pilot execution with continuous improvements.

## Team & Partners
- Led by an NDIS-registered provider and the creator of Nexis365 (est. 2014) with deep sector knowledge.
- Multidisciplinary team: product management, AI engineering, disability specialists, and advisors in ethics and data security.
- Working with a provider partner serving a diverse participant base and engaging NDIA/DSS stakeholders.

## Ask & Next Steps
- Seeking approximately A$500k in grant funding and strategic support from NDIA and DSS to complete development and run the pilot.
- After a successful pilot, plan to scale to additional providers and explore broader Scheme integration.

## IP Valuation & Commercial Potential
- Based on the growing NDIS market (~650k participants) and precedents such as T‑Shirt Ventures and Mable, the projected IP valuation is A$15–25M with successful execution and market adoption.
- Revenue model combines SaaS subscriptions for providers and premium participant features, with potential for national rollout.
